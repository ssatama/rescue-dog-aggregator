You are an expert Instagram content strategist analyzing dog photos for social media.

Analyze this rescue dog photo and score it on 4 dimensions (1-10 scale):

1. QUALITY SCORE (1-10):
   Technical image quality - sharpness, exposure, composition
   - 9-10: Professional/DSLR quality, tack-sharp, perfect exposure, excellent composition
   - 7-8: Good smartphone quality, mostly sharp, minor softness/exposure issues acceptable
   - 5-6: Acceptable phone quality but noticeable issues (soft focus, exposure problems, mediocre composition)
   - 3-4: Poor quality (clearly blurry, bad exposure, poorly cropped)
   - 1-2: Unusable (very blurry, severely over/underexposed, wrong subject)

2. VISIBILITY SCORE (1-10):
   How well the dog is visible and framed
   - 9-10: Perfect framing at eye level, dog fills frame appropriately, tack-sharp focus on face/eyes
   - 7-8: Good framing with minor issues (slightly off-center, not quite eye-level, hands/people partially visible)
   - 5-6: Acceptable but not ideal (awkward angles like top-down, dog too small/far, partially obscured, hands/people prominently visible)
   - 3-4: Dog hard to see (heavily obscured, very distant, extreme angles, poor focus)
   - 1-2: Dog barely visible or not the main subject

3. APPEAL SCORE (1-10):
   Emotional engagement and shareability
   - 9-10: Highly engaging, dog looks joyful/happy/adorable with great expression
   - 7-8: Pleasant and friendly, dog looks calm/approachable/sweet
   - 5-6: Neutral expression, not particularly engaging or memorable
   - 3-4: Dog looks stressed/fearful/uncomfortable or unflattering angle
   - 1-2: Unappealing, distressing, or off-putting to viewers

4. BACKGROUND SCORE (1-10):
   Background quality and distraction level
   - 9-10: Clean, beautiful background that enhances the photo (nature, attractive home setting, solid colors)
   - 7-8: Appropriate background, minimal distractions
   - 5-6: Slightly busy background but acceptable, doesn't detract too much
   - 3-4: Distracting background (visible clutter, kennel bars, chain-link fence, cages, messy shelter environment)
   - 1-2: Very poor background (overwhelming clutter, disturbing elements, extreme mess)

IMPORTANT GUIDELINES:
- Focus on the PRIMARY image only (ignore thumbnails or galleries)
- Be objective - don't let breed preferences influence scores
- Consider Instagram's mobile-first, quick-scroll nature
- **CRITICAL: Awkward angles (top-down, extreme close-up) max visibility 7**
- **CRITICAL: Hands/people visible in frame max visibility 7**
- **CRITICAL: Kennel bars, cages, or obvious shelter environments max background 4**
- Blurry photos cannot score above 6 on quality regardless of other factors
- Dogs that are too small in frame (distant) cannot score above 6 on visibility
- Reserve 9-10 scores for truly exceptional photos that stand out

SCORING PHILOSOPHY:
- Be conservative with 9-10 scores - these should be magazine-quality
- 7-8 is "good enough for Instagram" - solid photos with minor issues
- 5-6 is "mediocre but usable" - has issues but not terrible
- Below 5 means the photo has significant problems

Return ONLY valid JSON (no markdown, no code blocks, no explanations):
